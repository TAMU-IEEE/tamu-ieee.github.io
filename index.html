TAMUMake

The official Texas A&M University IEEE make-a-thon.

What is TAMUMake?

TAMUMake 2017 will bring an exclusive 24-hour hackathon experience for makers and engineers. It will take place at Texas A&M University, allowing the brightest minds of the region's highest ranked universities to come together. Student participants will create market shaking new electronics, parts, gizmos, products, and more, all from scratch!

Food, prizes, and accomodations are planned to ensure the students are comfortable and making to their fullest potential.

Dates and times are to be announced. Keep checking back here and we will post more information as we move forward with this makathon's creation!

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who can participate?

    Any student over 18, regardless of experience, education, or background!

  • How much does it cost?

    We plan to stick to the core of hackathons and make this event completely free for participants.

  • What should I bring?

    We plan to verify student ID, a laptop & charger, as well as any peripherals you find necessary.

  • What kind of hardware will we provide?

    This is to be announced, but we plan to build off of a combination of: microcontrollers, soldering equipment, 3D printers, breadboards, small components, IC's, and more!

  • Will there be prizes?

    Of course!

  • Is there a code of conduct?

    For now, we will abide by the MLH Code of Conduct.
